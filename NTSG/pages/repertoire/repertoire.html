Repertoire

In the vast realm of orchestral rep, how do you know what's a good pick and what will leave you questioning your career choice come concert time?

It's imperative to look at more than just notes & rhythms when selecting music. The technical demands of the piece need to be considered carefully. Far too often, the string technique required to execute--to perform--a piece is severely underestimated, setting everyone up for failure before they even begin.

Grade levels are not always a reliable indicator of difficulty.

Methods for determining difficulty very from publisher to publisher. On occasion, the results from these formulas fail to reflect the maturity required to approach a piece (example: Elgar's Nimrod is not truly appropriate for beginning orchestras, despite some arrangements being labeled "easy").
