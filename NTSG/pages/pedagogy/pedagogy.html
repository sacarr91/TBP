Pedagogy Overview

Left Hand Technique

  • Intonation
  • Pitch Accuracy
  • Ability to Shift
  • Vibrato

    • The left hand set up is studied extensively. Left hand dexterity affects shifting, vibrato, ability to play rapid passages, and pitch accuracy.

    It is important to develop a left hand technique free from tension. Excess tension leads to many bad habits and creates issues after as little as a few months of playing.

    Encourage students to release tension frequently. Avoid squeezing the instrument. Keep an eye on checkpoints as you monitor proper development of your students' technique.

    Right Hand Technique

  • Tone
  • Articulation
  • Note duration

    • Many people underestimate the importance of right hand technique in the face of left hand challenges, but to do so is a grave mistake. The bow is practically another instrument and is integral to characteristic string performance.

    Development of the bow hold and bow arm motion will affect facility at higher levels. Proper attention to the bow through all levels of learning is key to allowing players the ability to create quality tone and articulation.

    Music Reading

  • Pitch
  • Rhythm
  • Performance instructions
  • Sight-reading

    • Music reading is likely the easiest orchestral concept for the new-to-strings teacher, as mostly* it's not unique to the string world. *Except for alto clef.

    Reading Alto Clef: Just remember that the point in the middle of the clef points to "C" (like any movable C clef). You'll get the hang of it.

    Isolation & Combination

  • Guides scaffolding in the strings class
  • Essential at ALL levels of string teaching

    • The triangle diagram describes the break down and gradual recombination of string technique.

    In rehearsal, issues can be addressed by following this model. Start from the left side of the chart, addressing a single issue at its root. Once the simplest levels are understood, slowly increase complexity until all elements are again combined.

    Base of the Triangle: Body and instrument Posture is the foundation of all string skills. This must be well-established before anything else will work.
