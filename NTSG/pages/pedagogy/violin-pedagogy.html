    Posture & Position

  • Consider the chin rest as a "jaw rest"--eyes, nose, & front of chin should be facing the music **
  • Head should remain upright
  • Instrument scroll is ~45° forward from the shoulder
  • Sit or stand with physically balanced posture--weight evenly distributed & spine elongated
  • Elbow swings freely below instrument.

    • ** This description of head placement is only one school of thought, but it's the one I recommend for the heterogeneous classroom as it's the most ergonomic. There are pros who play with their head turned. However, teaching this way requires careful monitoring and adjustments more suited to the private lesson environment.

    Left Hand

  • Base knuckle of index finger touches side of fingerboard
  • Thumb lightly touches opposite the index
  • Tip of thumb sticks up just a little, like a "sunrise" (not a "flag")
  • Fingers curve to play on fingertips
  • Hand turns so that palm faces the player
  • Base of palm settles below largest knuckles ("straight wrist")
    • Fingernails facing player = success
    • Fingernails facing ceiling = collapsed wrist
