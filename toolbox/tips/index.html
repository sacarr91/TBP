pedagogy

  • Bass
  • Bass has three positions: ½, I, and thumb
  • Everything else - “put your finger here”
  • Rationale: Position work for bass doesn’t translate quickly to the other instruments as you can’t stay in a position for more than a whole step.
  • Contributed by: Mindy Curtis
  • "C natural / C#” → not high 2 & low 2
  • Utilize the names of the pitches so students don’t become dependent on numbers (and 1st position)
  • Violins: F-natural on the E string? How about 2nd position 4th finger instead
  • Shifting: Teach shifting to violin/viola via D/G 2 octave scale early.
  • Early familiarity = more fluent in upper positions → shifting in music becomes more approachable.

Scales

4th Finger/Closed Strings (violin/viola): use open strings when ascending, 4th finger when descending Optional: Encourage cello/bass to also “close” their open strings on the way down (play fingered note instead of open string) Using scales to build skills:
  • Half notes or long tones for scales for tuning
  • Utilize Harmony Director or drone
  • Scales in 3rds
Contributed by: Mindy Curtis

Kanban Board
What it is: Visual tool to help you track tasks that need to be completed How to implement: Create a physical board (as shown below) or a digital board with different columns to track items that need to be completed. Any columns can be created.
  • Optional column suggestions:
  • To Do, In Progress, Done
  • Due Today, This Week, Long Term
White Board Calendar
How to Implement: Create a large calendar on your white board using washi tape and expo markers Optional: This board shown below used wooden clips, a magnet, and printed name tags for the days of the week
Bass Rack Tip For 1/4 Size Basses:
How to implement: If your quarter size basses are too short to reach the top of the rack, add pool noodles (cut in half and to length) to give them some extra height
